Inclusion matters

Building an illustrative brand

Wealthfront is a financial services product that helps consumers make crucial investment decisions. We worked together to build out their illustration brand system and create 60+ illustrations for the product.

Starting blocks

Back in high school, I used WordPress It was one of the first places (other than Neopets) where I learned to do a bit of design, and got my feet wet with HTML and CSS. My friends and I figured it out on our own, even It was something that I did with my friends, and we kept each other motivated. Now, looking back, I could see how without that support system, it would have been easier to throw in the towel and give up. In particular, I can clearly see how looking at unrepresentative marketing imagery of young hacker dudes that one sees everywhere in tech could have played a role in making me feel unwelcome

Starting out, we explored many visual tones that expressed these values in different ways, and these are some of the key directions that emerged. In particular, we really gravitated towards a modernist approach, in that it tied in a sense of playfulness and ownable quirkiness while still feeling grounded and traditional.
